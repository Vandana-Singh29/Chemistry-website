Chapter 3: Classification of Elements and Periodicity in Properties

Explore the concept of Classification of Elements and Periodicity

1. Periodic Laws

  • Mendeleev’s Periodic Law: Properties of elements are periodic functions of their atomic masses.
  • Modern Periodic Law (Moseley): Properties of elements are periodic functions of their atomic numbers.

2. Classification of Elements

  • s-block: Groups 1 & 2. (ns¹ or ns² configuration)
  • p-block: Groups 13–18. (ns² np¹–⁶)
  • d-block (Transition elements): Groups 3–12. (n-1)d¹–¹⁰ ns¹–²
  • f-block (Inner-transition elements):
    • Lanthanoids: Ce (58) to Lu (71) → 4f filling
    • Actinoids: Th (90) to Lr (103) → 5f filling

3. Periodicity

Cause: Repetition of similar valence shell electronic configuration as atomic number increases.

4. Important Atomic Properties

Atomic Radius

Distance from nucleus to outermost electron shell.

Across a period: Decreases
Down a group: Increases

Ionic Radius

  • Cations < Neutral atom < Anions
  • In isoelectronic species: More positive charge = smaller radius

Ionisation Enthalpy (IE)

Energy to remove 1 mole of electrons from gaseous atoms.

Across period: Increases
Down group: Decreases

Order: IE₁ < IE₂ < IE₃

Electron Gain Enthalpy (EGE)

Energy change when a gaseous atom gains an electron.

  • Can be negative (exothermic) or positive (endothermic).
Across period: Becomes more negative
Down group: Becomes less negative

Electronegativity

Tendency to attract shared electrons.

Across period: Increases
Down group: Decreases

Valency

Number of electrons lost, gained or shared to achieve noble gas configuration.

Across period: Increases then decreases
Down group: Remains same

5. Metallic & Non-metallic Characters

Metallic Character

Tendency to lose electrons.

Across period: Decreases
Down group: Increases

Non-Metallic Character

Tendency to gain electrons.

Across period: Increases
Down group: Decreases

6. Reactivity Trends

Metals:

Reactivity decreases across a period.
Increases down a group.

Non-Metals:

Reactivity increases across a period.
Decreases down a group.

7. Solubility Trends

  • Alkali Metal Carbonates/Bicarbonates: Solubility increases down the group.
  • Alkaline Earth Metal Hydroxides/Sulphates:
    • Hydroxides: Solubility & basic strength ↑ down the group
    • Sulphates: Solubility ↓ down the group

8. Thermal Stability

  • Alkali metal carbonates: Stable on heating (except Li₂CO₃).
  • Alkaline earth carbonates: Decompose on heating; stability increases down the group.

9. Anomalous Properties of 2nd Period Elements

  • Small size
  • High electronegativity
  • Absence of d-orbitals
  • Form multiple bonds (C=C, C≡C, N≡N, etc.)
