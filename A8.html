Equilibrium

Summary of Key Concepts for Quick Revision

Equilibrium State

A state where the rate of formation of products equals the rate of formation of reactants.

For example, water can exist as solid, liquid, or gas:

H₂O(s) ⇌ H₂O(l) ⇌ H₂O(g)

Law of Chemical Equilibrium

The equilibrium constant (K) is the ratio of the concentration of products raised to their respective powers, over the concentration of reactants raised to their respective powers.

Kc = [C]c [D]d / [A]a [B]b

Le Chatelier's Principle

If a system at equilibrium is disturbed by a change in conditions (e.g., temperature, pressure), the system will shift to counteract the disturbance.

Equilibrium Constants

  • Kc: Equilibrium constant in terms of concentrations.
  • Kp: Equilibrium constant in terms of partial pressure.

Relation between Kp and Kc:

Kp = Kc (RT)Δn

Δn is the difference in moles of gaseous products and reactants.

Factors Affecting Equilibrium

  • Temperature
  • Pressure
  • Catalyst
  • Concentration of reactants/products

Acid-Base Concepts

  • Arrhenius Theory: Acids ionize to produce H⁺ ions in water; bases produce OH⁻ ions.
  • Bronsted-Lowry Theory: Acids donate protons (H⁺), and bases accept protons.
  • Lewis Theory: Acids accept electron pairs; bases donate electron pairs.

Ionic Product of Water

Kw = [H⁺] [OH⁻], which is 1 × 10−14 at 25°C.

pH and pOH

pH = −log[H⁺]
pH + pOH = 14

Buffer Solutions

Resist changes in pH upon dilution or addition of small amounts of acid/base.

Solubility Product (Ksp)

The equilibrium constant for a solid dissolving in a solvent, defined as the product of the concentrations of the ions, each raised to the power of their coefficients in the balanced equation.

Examples and Calculations

  • pH of Soft Drink: If the concentration of H⁺ is 3.8 × 10−3 M, the pH = 2.42.
  • Conjugate Acid-Base Pairs: HNO₂/NO₂⁻, CN⁻/HCN, H₂O/OH⁻.
  • Common Ion Effect: The suppression of ionization by adding a common ion.

Calculation of Equilibrium Constants

Using the relationship between Kc and Kp, or calculating ΔG using:

ΔG = −RT ln K

Example Problems

Calculation of Equilibrium Constant

For the reaction:

2A ⇌ B + C

If Kc = 2 × 10−3 and the concentration of A, B, and C are given as [A] = 3 × 10−4 M, [B] = 3 × 10−4 M, and [C] = 3 × 10−4 M, the reaction will proceed in the reverse direction since the reaction quotient Qc > Kc.

Conjugate Acid-Base Pairs

For HNO₂, the conjugate base is NO₂⁻.

For H₂O, the conjugate acid is H₃O⁺, and the conjugate base is OH⁻.

Le Chatelier’s Principle in Action

Effect of Pressure

Increasing pressure in a reaction involving gases typically shifts the equilibrium toward the side with fewer moles of gas.
