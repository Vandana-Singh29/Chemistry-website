Chapter 8: Redox Reactions

Overview of Redox Concepts and Reactions

Oxidation and Reduction

  • Oxidation: Increase in oxidation number (addition of oxygen, removal of hydrogen/electropositive element, loss of electrons).
  • Reduction: Decrease in oxidation number (addition of hydrogen/electronegative element, gain of electrons).

Oxidation Number (O.N.) Rules

  • O.N. of elements in their standard state (e.g., Cl₂, O₂) is zero.
  • Group 1 elements have O.N. = +1, Group 2 elements have O.N. = +2.
  • For monatomic ions, O.N. = charge on the ion.
  • O.N. of oxygen is usually -2, except in peroxides (-1) and superoxides (-½).
  • O.N. of hydrogen is +1, but -1 in metal hydrides.
  • O.N. of fluorine is always -1.
  • The sum of O.N. in a neutral compound = 0; in polyatomic ions, it equals the ion’s charge.

Stock Notation

Oxidation number is shown in Roman numerals in parentheses, e.g., Au(I)Cl (aurous chloride), Au(III)Cl₃ (auric chloride).

Redox Reactions

Involve a change in oxidation number of the substances involved.

  • Oxidizing agent: Causes oxidation (gains electrons).
  • Reducing agent: Causes reduction (loses electrons).

Balancing Redox Reactions

Oxidation Number Method

Example:

Cr2O72− + SO32− → Cr3+ + SO42−

Step-by-step method: Assign oxidation numbers, balance changes in oxidation states, add H⁺ to balance charges, and use water molecules to balance oxygen atoms.

Half-Reaction Method

Separate oxidation and reduction half-reactions, balance atoms, charges, and electrons.

Fe2+ + Cr2O72− → Fe3+ + Cr3+

Electrochemical Cells

Electrode Potential: The potential of an electrode when the concentration of all species is unity and the reaction is at 298K.

Standard Electrode Potential (SHE): Set to 0.00 V.

Cell Potential:

Ecell = Ecathode − Eanode

Key Concepts

  • Disproportionation: A reaction where the same species is both oxidized and reduced. Example:
    • P4 + 3OH− → PH3 + 3H2PO2−
  • Galvanization: Coating a less reactive metal (e.g., zinc) on a more reactive metal (e.g., iron) to prevent rusting.

Oxidizing and Reducing Agents

  • Oxidizing agents: MnO₄⁻, Cr₂O₇²⁻
  • Reducing agents: Fe²⁺, SO₃²⁻

Electrochemical Series

Metals arranged by their reducing power. Strongest reducing agents are at the bottom.

One Mark Questions

  • Oxidation: Increase in oxidation number.
  • Reduction: Decrease in oxidation number.
  • Disproportionation: Element simultaneously oxidized and reduced (e.g., P in P₄).
  • O.S. of sulfur in H₂SO₄: +6
  • Oxidation state of nitrogen in HNO₃: +5
  • Most reactive metal: Zn
  • Galvanization: Coating of iron with zinc to prevent rusting.
  • Standard Cell Potential: Ecell = Ecathode − Eanode
  • O.S. of oxygen in H₂O₂: -1
  • Redox process in NaCl formation: Na is oxidized, Cl is reduced.

Two Mark Questions

Balanced Redox Reactions:

MnO4⁻ + 5Fe²⁺ + 8H⁺ → Mn²⁺ + 5Fe³⁺ + 4H₂O
Cr2O72− + 6Fe²⁺ + 14H⁺ → 2Cr³⁺ + 6Fe³⁺ + 7H₂O

Reducing Agents:

  • Strongest: Na, Weakest: Ag.

Oxidation States:

  • KMnO₄: K = +1, Mn = +7, O = -2
  • K₂Cr₂O₇: K = +1, Cr = +6, O = -2
  • KClO₄: K = +1, Cl = +7, O = -2

Oxidation States of Na₂O₂ and OF₂:

  • Na₂O₂: Na = +1, O = -1
  • OF₂: F = -1, O = +2
