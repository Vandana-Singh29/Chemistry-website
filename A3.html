Chapter 2: Structure of Atom

Explore the fundamental concepts about the structure of an atom!

Structure of Atom

Atom: Smallest indivisible particle, composed of electrons, protons, and neutrons.

Particle Comparison

Charge Negative (−1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C) Positive (+1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C) Neutral

Cathode Ray Experiment

Discovered electrons, which are negatively charged and travel in straight lines. Their behavior changes in the presence of electric or magnetic fields.

Charge to Mass Ratio of Electron

The ratio is 1.7588 × 10¹¹ C/kg, determined by J.J. Thomson.

Millikan’s Oil Drop Experiment

Determined the charge of an electron to be -1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C.

Discovery of Protons

Via canal rays, protons carry a positive charge and depend on the gas in the cathode ray tube.

Discovery of Neutrons

Discovered by Chadwick in 1932, through bombardment of beryllium with alpha particles.

Atomic Number (Z) and Mass Number (A)

Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons.

Mass Number (A): Sum of protons and neutrons.

Models of Atom

Thomson’s Model

Uniform positive charge with electrons embedded (Plum Pudding Model). Discarded due to Rutherford’s α-particle scattering experiment.

Rutherford’s Model

The atom has a small, dense nucleus with electrons revolving around it. However, it couldn’t explain electron energy distributions.

Isotopes & Isobars

Isotopes

Atoms of the same element with different mass numbers (e.g., ¹H, ²H, ³H).

Isobars

Atoms with the same mass number but different atomic numbers (e.g., ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁰Ar).

Isoelectronic Species

Species with the same number of electrons.

Electromagnetic Radiation

Electromagnetic radiation consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields, and it can travel through a vacuum.

  • Wavelength (λ)
  • Frequency (ν)
  • Velocity (v): In a vacuum, the velocity is 3 × 10⁸ m/s.

Quantum Theory (Planck)

Energy is emitted in discrete packets called quanta.

The energy of a quantum: E = hν, where h is Planck’s constant.

Black Body Radiation

Black bodies absorb and emit all frequencies of radiation.

Photoelectric Effect

Light of a certain frequency can eject electrons from a metal surface.

  • Threshold frequency: The minimum frequency required to eject electrons.

Bohr’s Model

Electrons occupy specific orbits around the nucleus with fixed energies. However, it couldn’t explain finer details of the hydrogen spectrum or atoms with more than one electron.

Dual Behavior of Matter

de Broglie’s Hypothesis

Matter exhibits both particle and wave properties.

Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle

It is impossible to precisely measure both the position and momentum of a particle at the same time.

Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics describes microscopic particles with both wave and particle properties, contrasting with classical mechanics.
