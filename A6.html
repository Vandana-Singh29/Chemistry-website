Chapter 5: States of Matter

Short Notes for Quick Revision

🔹 Basics

  • State Change: Result of intermolecular forces, interaction energy & thermal energy.
  • Gas Properties: Pressure (P), Volume (V), Temperature (T), number of moles (n), viscosity, specific heat.

🔹 Gas Laws

Law Relationship Formula
Boyle’s LawP ∝ 1/V (T, n constant)P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
Charles’s LawV ∝ T (P constant)V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
Gay-Lussac’s LawP ∝ T (V constant)P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂
Avogadro’s LawV ∝ n (T, P constant)V₁/n₁ = V₂/n₂
Ideal Gas EquationCombines all abovePV = nRT
Dalton’s LawTotal pressure = sum of partial pressuresPₜ = P₁ + P₂ + P₃ + …

🔹 Kinetic Molecular Theory

  • Gas = many small, fast, random particles.
  • Negligible volume and no attraction.
  • Collisions are perfectly elastic.
  • Pressure due to collisions.
  • Avg KE ∝ Absolute temperature.

🔹 Real vs Ideal Gases

  • Ideal Gas: Obeys PV = nRT always.
  • Real Gas: Deviates at high P, low T due to attraction and finite volume.

🔹 Van der Waals Equation

(P + a(n²/V²))(V − nb) = nRT

🔹 Compressibility Factor (Z)

Z = PV / nRT
  • Ideal Gas: Z = 1
  • Real Gas: Z < 1 (attraction dominates), Z > 1 (repulsion dominates)

🔹 Critical Constants

  • Critical Temperature (Tc): Max T for gas liquefaction.
  • Critical Pressure (Pc): P needed to liquefy at Tc.
  • Critical Volume (Vc): Volume of 1 mole at Tc and Pc.

🔹 Liquids

Property Definition
Vapour PressurePressure by vapour in equilibrium at a given T. Increases with T.
Surface TensionForce per unit length acting along liquid surface. Decreases with T.
Viscosity (η)Internal resistance to flow. Decreases with ↑T, increases with ↑P.
Supercooled LiquidsCooled below freezing point without solidifying.
Elastic CollisionNo energy loss, only energy transfer.

🔹 One Mark Q&A Highlights

  • Z for Ideal Gas: 1
  • Critical Temp: Above which gas can't liquefy
  • He in Balloons: Non-flammable
  • Lowest Gas Volume: 0 at 0 K
  • Molar Volume: 22.4 L at 0°C & 1 atm
  • Boiling Pt at Altitude: Decreases due to low pressure
  • Effect of T on Viscosity/Surface Tension: Both ↓ with ↑T

🔹 Quick Equations & Units

ρ = PM / RT
  • Pressure Units: 1 atm = 101325 Pa = 1.01325 bar
  • Van der Waals: a → atm·L²/mol², b → L/mol

🔹 Miscellaneous Short Notes

  • Capillary rise & droplet shape: Due to surface tension.
  • Liquids diffuse slower: Due to stronger intermolecular forces than gases.
  • Density Order: Depends on P/T ratio.
  • Aqueous Tension: Vapor pressure of water at given T.
  • Air Balloon Expansion: Low external P at altitude → balloon expands.
