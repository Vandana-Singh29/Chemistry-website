UNIT 1: Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

What is Chemistry?

Study of composition, structure, and properties of matter.

Known as the science of atoms and molecules.

Branches of Chemistry

  • Organic: Carbon compounds
  • Inorganic: All elements except carbon
  • Physical: Laws and theories
  • Analytical: Qualitative & quantitative analysis
  • Industrial: Industrial processes
  • Biochemistry: Reactions in living organisms
  • Nuclear: Nuclear reactions (fission, fusion, etc.)

Properties of Matter

  • Physical Properties: Observable without changing composition (e.g., color, boiling point)
  • Chemical Properties: Observed with a change in composition (e.g., flammability)

Measurement & SI Units

  • SI System: 7 Base Units (e.g., metre, kilogram, kelvin)
  • Mass: Constant, measured in kg
  • Weight: Varies with gravity
  • Volume: m³ or dm³ (1 L = 1 dm³)
  • Density: mass/volume (kg/m³ or g/cm³)
  • Temperature: °C, °F, K (K = °C + 273.15)
  • Scientific Notation: N × 10ⁿ

Accuracy, Precision & Significant Figures

  • Accuracy: Closeness to true value
  • Precision: Repeatability of measurements
  • Significant Figures: Digits that reflect accuracy
  • Rounding Rules:
    • 5 → round up
    • <5 → leave unchanged
    • 5 → round to even

Dimensional Analysis

Used for unit conversions (e.g., 1 inch = 2.54 cm)

Chemical Types

  • Element: Pure, can’t be broken down
  • Compound: Elements combined in fixed ratio
  • Mixture: Variable ratio, components retain identity
    • Homogeneous: Uniform (e.g., saltwater)
    • Heterogeneous: Non-uniform (e.g., sand & water)

Laws of Chemical Combination

  • Law of Conservation of Mass: Mass can't be created or destroyed
  • Law of Definite Proportions: A compound always has same composition
  • Law of Multiple Proportions: Simple ratio of masses in multiple compounds
  • Gay Lussac’s Law: Gases react in simple volume ratios
  • Avogadro’s Law: Equal volumes of gases = equal molecules at same T & P

Atomic Theory (Dalton)

  • Matter is made of atoms
  • Atoms of same element are identical
  • Atoms combine in whole numbers
  • Atoms cannot be created/destroyed

Atoms & Molecules

  • Atom: Smallest particle of element
  • Molecule: Smallest unit of a compound
  • Atomicity: Number of atoms in a molecule

Mass Concepts

  • Atomic Mass Unit (amu): 1/12 of C-12 atom
  • Atomic Mass: Avg. mass relative to C-12
  • Molecular Mass: Sum of atomic masses in molecule
  • Gram Atomic/Molecular Mass: Mass in grams equal to atomic/molecular mass
  • Formula Mass: For ionic compounds

Mole Concept

  • 1 mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles
  • Molar Mass: Mass of 1 mole
  • Molar Volume: Volume of 1 mole gas at STP = 22.4 L

Percentage Composition

% element = (mass of element / molar mass) × 100

Empirical & Molecular Formulas

  • Empirical: Simplest ratio
  • Molecular: Actual number of atoms
  • n = Molecular mass / Empirical mass

Chemical Equations

Reactants → Products

Limiting Reagent: Determines amount of product formed

Concentration of Solutions

  • Mass % = mass of solute / total mass × 100
  • Volume % = volume of solute / total volume × 100
  • Molarity (M) = moles of solute / volume of solution (L)
  • M₁V₁ = M₂V₂
  • Molality (m) = moles of solute / kg of solvent
  • Mole Fraction (x) = moles of component / total moles

Key One-Mark Concepts

  • 1 mole = Avogadro’s number
  • SI unit of density = kg/m³
  • Limiting reagent = Used up first in a reaction
  • Mole fraction = moles of one component / total moles
