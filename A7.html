Chapter 6: Thermodynamics

Summary Notes for Quick Revision

1. Thermodynamics

Study of energy transformations and relationships between heat, work, and internal energy.

2. System & Surroundings

  • System: Part of the universe under study.
  • Surroundings: Everything else outside the system.

3. State Function & State of System

  • Described by properties like P, V, T, etc.
  • State Functions: Depend only on state, not the path (e.g., P, V, T, E, H, S).

4. Extensive vs. Intensive Properties

  • Extensive: Depends on quantity (e.g., mass, volume, enthalpy).
  • Intensive: Independent of amount (e.g., temperature, pressure, density).

5. Internal Energy (U)

ΔU = q + w

Total energy of the system. It is a state function and extensive property.

6. Heat (q)

Energy exchanged due to temperature difference.

Unit: Joule (J) or Calorie (1 Cal = 4.18 J)

7. First Law of Thermodynamics

ΔU = q + w
w = –PΔV

Law of conservation of energy.

8. Enthalpy (H)

ΔH = ΔU + PΔV

At constant pressure, heat exchanged is enthalpy change: qp = ΔH

9. Relation Between ΔH and ΔU

ΔH = ΔU + ngRT

ng = change in moles of gas

10. Reaction Types

  • Exothermic: ΔH < 0
  • Endothermic: ΔH > 0

11–13. Types of Enthalpy

  • Formation (ΔHf)
  • Combustion (ΔHc)
  • Neutralization
  • Solution
  • Atomization (ΔHa)
  • Hydration (ΔHhyd)
  • Fusion (ΔHfus)
  • Vaporization (ΔHvap)
  • Sublimation: ΔHsub = ΔHfus + ΔHvap

14. Hess's Law

Total enthalpy change is path-independent:

ΔH = ΔH₁ + ΔH₂ + ...

15. Bond Enthalpy

Energy to break one mole of bonds in gaseous state.

ΔHr = BE(Reactants) – BE(Products)

16–17. Spontaneity

  • Spontaneous: Occurs by itself.
  • Driven by:
    1. Minimum energy
    2. Maximum entropy

18. Entropy (S)

Measure of disorder.

ΔS = qrev / T

19. Entropy & Spontaneity

If total entropy change:

ΔStotal = ΔSsystem + ΔSsurroundings > 0

Then the process is spontaneous.

20. Second Law of Thermodynamics

In spontaneous processes, entropy of universe increases.

21. Gibbs Free Energy (G)

G = H – TS
  • ΔG < 0 → Spontaneous
  • ΔG > 0 → Non-spontaneous

22. Temperature & Spontaneity

  • Endothermic may be spontaneous at high T
  • Exothermic may be non-spontaneous at high T

23–25. ΔG & Equilibrium

ΔG° = ΔGf°(products) – ΔGf°(reactants)
ΔG° = –2.303 RT log K
